# How to Use Ireland's Property Price Register

Ireland's Property Price Register (PPR) is a public database containing details of every residential property sale in Ireland since 2010. Understanding how to use this valuable resource can help you make informed property decisions, whether you're buying, selling, or simply researching the market.

## What is the Property Price Register?

The PPR was established in 2010 following the housing market crash to bring transparency to Irish property transactions. It contains:

- **Sale price** of every residential property
- **Address** of the property
- **Sale date** (when the transaction completed)
- **Property description** (new/second-hand, VAT exclusive indicator)

## Understanding the Data

### Price Indicators

**Not Full Market Price:**
Some sales are marked as "Not Full Market Price". These include:
- Sales between family members
- Portfolio sales
- Sales at below market value
- Distressed sales

For market research, focus on **full market price** sales only.

### Property Descriptions

- **New** - First sale of a newly built property
- **Second-hand** - Previously occupied property
- **VAT Exclusive** - Price doesn't include VAT (usually new builds)

## Tips for Property Research

1. **Look at trends, not single sales** - One high/low sale doesn't define a market
2. **Compare like-with-like** - Match property type, size, condition
3. **Consider the date** - Older sales may not reflect current market
4. **Check multiple sources** - Cross-reference with MyHome, Daft listings
5. **Understand local factors** - Schools, transport, amenities affect prices

## Common Questions

**Q: Why don't I see my address?**
A: The PPR only includes sales since 2010. If your property hasn't sold since then, it won't appear.

**Q: Is the data accurate?**
A: Yes - the data comes directly from Revenue and is verified against stamp duty returns.

**Q: How often is it updated?**
A: Sales appear in the PPR after the transaction completes and stamp duty is paid, typically 1-2 months after the sale date.
